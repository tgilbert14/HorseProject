2) Physical Soundness

3) Performance Potential / Proof

4) Price-Value Edge

9–10: clear under-market opportunity, high upside per dollar

5–6: market-correct pricing, no major edge

1–4: expensive for risk/quality profile

5) Mindset & Trainability

9–10: professional attitude, focused, highly trainable

7–8: mostly good behavior, manageable quirks

5–6: average attitude, inconsistent focus

1–4: difficult temperament, reliability concerns

Selection Guide — What the factors mean

Pedigree (≈ 30% of score)

  • Sire AEI / CI — Average Earnings Index measures a sire's progeny earnings against the average (1.0). Elite sires consistently exceed 2.0. CI compares against the mares he's covered.
  • Dam quality — A productive dam (multiple winners, black-type) is statistically the strongest single predictor in many studies. Look at the second dam too.
  • Black-type within 3 generations — Italicized/bolded names in the catalogue. The closer up, the better.
  • Nicking — Statistical compatibility of sire line × broodmare sire line. A+ nicks have produced disproportionate stakes winners.
  • Dosage Index — Speed-vs-stamina balance derived from chef-de-race ancestors. ~2–4 favors speed, <1 favors stamina/turf.
  • Inbreeding — Light inbreeding to superior ancestors (3×4, 4×4) can concentrate quality; heavy inbreeding (>6%) raises risk.

Conformation (≈ 20%)

  • Balance — Shoulder length ≈ hip length; horse should look "in three equal parts."
  • Shoulder — A long, sloping shoulder (~45°) gives stride length and absorbs concussion.
  • Hindquarter — The engine. Look for muscle, length from hip to hock, and a well-let-down hock.
  • Cannon bone — Short, dense, and substantial. ≥8" circumference for a yearling colt is a common benchmark.
  • Pasterns — 45–50°. Too upright = concussion injuries; too sloped = soft-tissue strain.
  • Knees — Should be flat from the side, not "back at the knee" (predisposes to chips) or "over at the knee."
  • Walk — A free, overstepping walk strongly correlates with athletic gallop.

Veterinary (≈ 15%)

  • Repository X-rays at major sales — review with a vet. OCD lesions in stifles/hocks are red flags.
  • Throat scope — Laryngeal Grade I–II is standard; Grade III/IV signals roaring risk.
  • Heart, wind, eyes — basic soundness checks.

Performance (raced horses)

  • Earnings, speed figures (Beyer/Timeform), highest class won, and soundness history.
  • For unraced yearlings/weanlings this section is skipped automatically.

Auction / Value

  • The "Value Score" on the list view = Overall Score ÷ price (in $10k units). It surfaces under-the-radar horses whose quality outpaces their reserve.

Breeding

  • Mare: produce record > her own race record. An older mare with multiple winners is more valuable than a young one with no foals.
  • Stallion: % stakes winners from runners is the cleanest quality metric; book size and stud fee indicate market belief.
  • Cross / nick: switch the mode toggle to a breeding mode and the cross/nick fields will be weighted heavily.

Five Pillars Workflow (Recommended)

  • Score each pillar 1–10 using the rubric in the Five Pillars card.
  • Use Estimated mode when reviewing historical horses or candidates with incomplete private vet/conformation data.
  • Use Strict verified mode when you only want scores built from direct entered evidence (no inferred estimates).
  • Interpret outputs: BUY = strong candidate, SHORTLIST = re-inspect, PASS = risk/value mismatch.
  • Watch Confidence %: low confidence means you need more direct inputs before final bid decisions.

This tool is a decision aid, not a substitute for a qualified bloodstock agent and veterinarian. Always vet in person before bidding.

Scoring Categories

Category Auction Weight What it measures
Pedigree ~30% Sire AEI, dam produce, black-type proximity, nicking grade, dosage, inbreeding
Conformation ~22% 10 structural traits rated 1–10 (balance, shoulder, hip, cannon, knees, pasterns, hooves, neck, topline, walk)
Veterinary ~15% Repository X-rays, throat scope grade, heart, wind, eyes
Performance ~8% Starts/wins/places, earnings, Beyer/Timeform speed figure, class level, soundness
Auction / Price ~10% Reserve vs market estimate (generates Value Score), consignor reputation
Physical ~5% Height, girth, cannon circumference vs ideal ranges
Temperament ~5% Attitude, trainability
Foaling Date ~5% Jan–Mar foals favored (universal Jan 1 birthday rule)
Breeding 0% (auction) Mare produce record / stallion progeny stats (weighted higher in breeding modes)

Key Features

  • Real Horse Presets — Load verified public-record data for 22 famous horses (Secretariat, Frankel, Winx, etc.) with one click
  • Live Scoring — Overall score updates in real time as you fill the form
  • Value Score — Overall Score ÷ price, surfacing quality below market price
  • Side-by-side Compare — Select 2–4 horses; best/worst values highlighted automatically
  • CSV Export — Spreadsheet-ready export including all sub-scores for Excel or Google Sheets
  • JSON Backup — Full backup and restore of all records
  • Custom Weights — Adjust how much each category counts per evaluation mode (⚖ Weights button)
  • Selection Guide tab — Built-in reference explaining every factor and the research behind it

Data & Privacy

All data is stored in your browser's localStorage. Nothing is sent to any server. Use Export JSON regularly as a backup — clearing browser data will erase saved horses.

Preset Data Sources

Preset horses use public-record data only: Jockey Club, Weatherbys, Equibase, Racing Post, BHA, and publicly advertised stud farm fee schedules. Conformation, vet findings, and temperament are intentionally blank — those require in-person evaluation.

⚠️ This tool is a structured decision aid, not a substitute for a qualified bloodstock agent and veterinarian. Always conduct an in-person inspection and independent veterinary review before bidding at auction.
