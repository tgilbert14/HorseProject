Moderator sheet

Keep this in view during the session. 30 minutes, 1:1, screen-share, think-aloud.
🐎

The one question: when a genuinely-new owner uses this, do they come away more confident and competent — or more confused (or falsely confident)?

Session flow

0–4 minWarm-up. “No wrong answers — I’m testing the tool, not you. Please think out loud.” Ask: their interest in ownership; ever looked at a horse to buy; how it felt.
4–24 minFive tasks (below). Hand over the URL, then stay quiet and take notes on the log sheet.
24–30 minDebrief (questions below), then send the survey.

The five tasks — read each aloud, then wait

  1. Learn (cold open). “Spend two minutes getting a feel for this. What do you think it’s for?”
  2. Legends. “Figure out what makes a great horse’s page great. Tell me one thing you learned.”
  3. Evaluate. “A friend offers you a share in a yearling. Load a famous horse as a stand-in, or enter one — does this look like a good horse, and how sure are you?”
  4. Price. “It’s at Keeneland September for $60,000 — normal price? Now imagine it’s in Book 1.”
  5. Take it to your agent. “You want an expert’s second opinion. Get something you could send them.”
Do
  • Stay silent while they explore — let them struggle
  • Ask “what did you expect there?” when they pause
  • Echo their words back, never yours
Don’t
  • Point, hint, or rescue them
  • Explain a feature they didn’t find
  • Ask leading “wasn’t that helpful?” questions

What to watch for (the assumptions under test)

A2Does the checklist teach?Can they explain a pillar back, unprompted?
A3Score read as a lean, not a verdict?Do they over-trust the number, or say “it’s just my inputs”?
A4Price check feels useful?An audible “oh, that’s helpful”? Do they get the book nuance?
A5Data entry not too heavy?Where do they stall, sigh, or give up?
A6Report worth sending?Would they send it? To whom?

Debrief — the money questions

  • “In one sentence, what is this for?”   “What would you tell a friend buying their first share?”
  • “Did anything make you trust it more, or less?”   “Most confusing or annoying moment?”
  • “Would you use this again before a real purchase — why / why not?”
Green if they can explain a pillar (A2) + read the score as a lean (A3) + would use it again + volunteer the report/price as the best part. Red if they feel no more capable, act on the score as a verdict, or wouldn’t reuse it or send the report. 1 / 3 · Moderator
